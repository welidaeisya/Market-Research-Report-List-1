<p><strong>The Dental Restorative Material Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Amalgam</li><li>Resin Based Composite</li><li>Glass Ionomer</li><li>Resin Modified Glass Ionomer</li><li>Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental restorative material market comprises several types, each offering unique properties for dental applications. Amalgam is a durable, metal-based alloy used for filling cavities. Resin-based composites provide aesthetic advantages with tooth-like appearance and good bonding properties. Glass ionomer releases fluoride and adheres well, making it suitable for areas requiring moisture resistance. Resin-modified glass ionomer combines benefits of both glass ionomer and resin composites. Porcelain-fused-to-metal restorations offer strength and aesthetics, widely used in crowns and bridges for enhanced durability.</p></p>
