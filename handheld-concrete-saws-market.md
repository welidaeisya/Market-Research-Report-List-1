<p><p>The handheld concrete saws market features two primary blade types: abrasive blades and diamond blades. Abrasive blades utilize a composite material for cutting, making them suitable for soft concrete and other masonry surfaces. They are cost-effective but wear out quickly. In contrast, diamond blades incorporate diamond segments for superior cutting performance, enabling them to tackle tougher materials like reinforced concrete. Diamond blades provide longer lifespan and better precision, making them increasingly popular in the construction and renovation industries.</p></p>
