<p><p>Upper limb orthotics can be categorized into two main types: dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to allow movement while providing support, often incorporating flexible materials and mechanisms to facilitate rehabilitation and enhance functional abilities. In contrast, static orthotics are primarily intended to immobilize or stabilize a limb, ensuring proper alignment and promoting healing by limiting motion. Both types play crucial roles in the management of upper limb injuries and conditions, tailored to meet individual patient needs.</p></p>
